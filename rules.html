Rules

Here are the rules of Texas Hold 'Em Poker:

  1. Each player receives two private cards ("hole cards").
  2. Five community cards are dealt face-up in stages: three for the Flop, one for the Turn, and one for the River.
  3. Players use any combination of their hole cards and the community cards to make the best five-card hand.
  4. There are four betting rounds: Pre-Flop (after hole cards), Flop, Turn, and River.
  5. On each turn, players can Fold (quit), Call (match the bet), or Raise (increase the bet).
  6. Before cards are dealt, the two players (if there are more than three) left of the dealer post mandatory bets: the small blind and big blind.
  7. After each round, the small and big blinds rotate amongst the players clock-wise.
  8. After the final betting round, if more than one player remains, a showdown occurs and the best hand wins the pot.
  9. The computer acts as the dealer, handling cards and bets.
